Jeera Rice

Description

Jeera Rice is a simple yet flavorful Indian rice dish.It's made with fluffy basmati rice cooked with aromatic cumin seeds and often other whole spices like cardamom and cloves. The dish is fragrant with a warm, earthy cumin aroma and pairs perfectly with curries or dals.

Ingredients

  • Rice: Basmati rice is preferred for its long grains and fluffy texture.
  • Fat: Ghee (clarified butter) or oil for adding richness.
  • Cumin seeds (Jeera): The star ingredient that gives the dish its name and flavor.
  • Optional whole spices: Bay leaf,cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick - these add extra depth of flavor.
  • Salt: to taste
  • For garnish: Fresh coriander leaves.

Steps to make

  1. Rinse the rice: Wash your basmati rice in cool water until the water runs clear. Soaking for 20-30 minutes (optional) can further improve texture.
  2. Heat the fat: In a pot or pressure cooker, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.
  3. Temper the spices: Add cumin seeds and any optional whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, etc.) to the hot fat. Fry until the cumin releases its fragrant aroma.
  4. Saut√© the rice (optional): You can briefly saute the rinsed rice in the hot oil for a minute to enhance its flavor.
  5. Add water and salt: Pour in water (typically 1.5-2 parts water to 1 part rice) and add salt to taste.
  6. Bring to a boil and simmer: Increase heat to bring the water to a boil. Then, reduce heat to low, cover the pot or pressure cooker, and simmer the rice according to your chosen method:
    • Stovetop method: Simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and all the water is absorbed. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.
    • Pressure cooker method: Cook for 2-3 whistles on medium flame. Let the pressure release naturally for 5-10 minutes before opening the cooker. Fluff the rice gently.
  7. Garnish (optional): Stir in chopped fresh coriander leaves for a touch of green and freshness before serving.
